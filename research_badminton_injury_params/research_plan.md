## Main Research Question
What published evidence links these landing/form parameters to injury risk in badminton and related landing biomechanics: hip trajectory, knee flexion, foot alignment, and landing pitch; how do they interact; and what injuries and severities are reported?

## Subtopics

1. **Knee flexion + landing pitch biomechanics and impact loading**
   - Expected: associations between sagittal-plane landing posture (knee angle, foot/ankle pitch) and ground reaction force, knee/ankle joint loading, ACL/PFJ/patellar tendon stress.

2. **Foot alignment (frontal/transverse plane) and lower-limb injury risk**
   - Expected: evidence on foot progression angle, pronation/supination, rearfoot/forefoot alignment, dynamic knee valgus coupling, and links to ankle sprain, ACL, PF pain.

3. **Hip trajectory/control and proximal-to-distal injury mechanisms**
   - Expected: evidence for hip flexion/adduction/internal rotation control effects on knee valgus and downstream loading; relevance to landing and cutting in racket sports.

4. **Parameter interactions and severity/outcome mapping**
   - Expected: integrated model of how combinations (e.g., low knee flexion + adverse pitch + poor alignment + poor hip control) amplify risk; injury types and severity grading (acute severe vs overuse).

## Synthesis Method
- Compile evidence by parameter first.
- Extract interaction statements and mechanistic pathways.
- Map to injuries (ankle sprain, ACL/MCL, patellar tendinopathy, PF pain, meniscal/cartilage injury, stress injuries).
- Summarize relative severity: high acute injury risk vs moderate chronic overload risk, including uncertainty where direct badminton evidence is sparse.
