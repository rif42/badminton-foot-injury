## Hip Trajectory/Control and Downstream Injury Risk

### Key findings
- Recurrent risk chain: low hip flexion + high hip adduction/internal rotation + trunk lean -> higher dynamic knee valgus and knee abduction load.
- Prospective data identify lower hip flexion at contact and weaker hip external rotators among factors associated with future ACL injury.
- In badminton, female players show landing patterns (lower hip flexion, greater valgus) consistent with higher ACL-risk mechanics.

- ACL pathway is high severity: surgery common, long return-to-sport timeline, rerupture risk, and later osteoarthritis burden.
