## Foot Alignment and Injury Risk

### Key findings
- Toe-in landing tends to increase ACL-risk biomechanics (higher hip adduction, knee internal rotation/abduction).
- Badminton case data show lateral ankle sprain can occur with plantarflexed + internally rotated + everted forefoot contact.
- Badminton lunge evidence suggests toe-out can reduce some ankle eversion/shear loading markers vs toe-in.
- Static foot posture alone is a weak/inconsistent predictor for some overuse outcomes (e.g., PF pain), suggesting interaction with whole-chain mechanics is key.

### Evidence snippets
> “landing in the toe-in position increased peak hip adduction, knee internal rotation angles and moments … and peak knee abduction angle”

Source: https://jeo-esska.springeropen.com/articles/10.1186/s40634-016-0049-1

> “At foot strike, the ankle joint was 2° everted, 33° plantarflexed, and 18° internally rotated.”

> “A forefoot landing posture with a plantarflexed and internally rotated ankle joint configuration could incite an acute lateral ankle sprain injury in badminton.”

Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33744478/

> “Toe-out landings significantly reduced ankle eversion angles, eversion moments, anterior-posterior shear forces, and joint impulse”

Source: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s13102-025-01393-y

> “very limited evidence that a pronated foot posture was a risk factor for patellofemoral pain development”

Source: http://www.jfootankleres.com/content/7/1/55

### Severity linkage
- Can drive severe acute injuries (lateral ankle sprain; ACL-loading patterns) and chronic overload patterns depending on exposure and whole-chain control.
