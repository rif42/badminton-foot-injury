## Knee Flexion + Landing Pitch (Ankle at Initial Contact)

### Key findings
- Lower knee flexion at initial contact (stiffer landing) is repeatedly associated with higher impact loading and less energy absorption.
- In badminton, female players showed lower hip flexion, greater knee valgus, and greater ankle dorsiflexion during single-leg landing, consistent with higher ACL-risk mechanics.
- Landing with greater ankle plantarflexion at initial contact (forefoot strategy) is associated in some studies with reduced knee frontal/transverse loading surrogates.
- Cutting/landing literature flags near-knee-extension and rearfoot-style landings as higher-risk mechanics.
